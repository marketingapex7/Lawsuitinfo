<section id="state-paraquat-context">
<h2>What stands out about Paraquat litigation in Illinois?</h2>
<p>Illinois is uniquely the host state for the federal paraquat MDL. MDL-3004 (<em>In re: Paraquat Products Liability Litigation</em>) sits in the Southern District of Illinois (East St. Louis division), before Chief Judge Nancy J. Rosenstengel. Illinois plaintiffs file in their home federal district and stay in-state for coordinated proceedings; out-of-state plaintiffs are transferred in by the JPML.</p>
<p>Illinois is also one of the largest documented paraquat-use environments in the country. The USDA NASS 2018 chemical-use surveys ranked Illinois second nationally in both corn (about 11.0 million acres) and soybean (about 10.8 million acres) planted acreage, both crops with widespread paraquat use as a burndown herbicide.</p>
<p>As of mid-2026, a review of Illinois Department of Agriculture enforcement records and Illinois Attorney General press releases did not surface a state-level paraquat action against Syngenta or Chevron; this is an absence-of-evidence statement rather than a guarantee.</p>

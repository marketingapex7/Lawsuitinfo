<section id="state-overview">
<h2>What Missouri residents should know</h2>
<p>Missouri residents are not necessarily limited to filing only in Missouri state court. Many mass tort claims may be evaluated by national firms, filed in federal court, coordinated through MDL proceedings, or handled through another legal process.</p>
<p>State law may still matter for deadlines, damages, claim evaluation, and certain procedural issues.</p>
</section>

<section id="state-paraquat-context">
<h2>What stands out about Paraquat litigation in Missouri?</h2>
<p>Missouri is geographically and structurally close to the federal paraquat MDL: MDL-3004 sits in East St. Louis, Illinois &mdash; immediately across the Mississippi from St. Louis &mdash; making the MDL a practical venue for Missouri claimants. Missouri's federal paraquat cases (filed in the Eastern or Western District of Missouri) are transferred by the JPML into MDL-3004 before Chief Judge Nancy J. Rosenstengel.</p>
<p>Missouri is also a documented paraquat-use state. Per the USDA NASS 2018 Soybean Chemical Use Survey, Missouri ranked among the top soybean states with about 5.9 million planted acres. Missouri is also included in NASS cotton chemical-use surveys (about 315,000 cotton acres in 2021). Paraquat is used in both crops as a burndown and pre-harvest desiccant.</p>
<p>Missouri pesticide regulation is governed by the Missouri Pesticide Use Act (Ch. 281 RSMo). As of mid-2026, a review of Missouri Attorney General press releases and Missouri Department of Agriculture enforcement records did not surface a state-level paraquat action against Syngenta or Chevron; this is an absence-of-evidence statement rather than a guarantee. Missouri's personal-injury statute of limitations is unusually long &mdash; generally five years (Mo. Rev. Stat. &sect; 516.120).</p>

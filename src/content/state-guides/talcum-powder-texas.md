<section id="state-overview">
<h2>What Texas residents should know</h2>
<p>Texas residents with talcum powder claims are generally not limited to Texas state court. Most federal ovarian-cancer cases are coordinated in the multidistrict litigation, <strong>MDL-2738</strong>, before Judge Michael A. Shipp in the U.S. District Court for the District of New Jersey, which held about 68,029 pending actions as of June 1, 2026 — the largest active MDL by pending actions.</p>
<p>Texas law still matters: the state's filing deadline, discovery rule, damages rules, and procedural law can shape an individual claim even when the case is litigated in the MDL or in state court.</p>
</section>

<section id="state-talcum-powder-context">
<h2>Texas and the Red River talc bankruptcy</h2>
<p>Texas has a distinctive place in the talc litigation: it is where Johnson &amp; Johnson made its third and final 'Texas Two-Step' bankruptcy attempt. J&amp;J's subsidiary Red River Talc LLC filed a prepackaged Chapter 11 &mdash; built around a proposed roughly $9 billion plan &mdash; in the U.S. Bankruptcy Court for the Southern District of Texas, and Judge Christopher Lopez denied confirmation on March 31, 2025. With that route closed, Texas residents' federal talc cases proceed in MDL-2738 in the District of New Jersey, and individual cases are again being tried.</p>

<section id="filing-process">
<h2>How Texas claims proceed</h2>
<p>Most Texas federal talc cases are filed in or transferred to MDL-2738 in the District of New Jersey for coordinated proceedings, while many ovarian-cancer and mesothelioma cases are also tried in state courts. Johnson & Johnson's three "Texas Two-Step" bankruptcy attempts were all rejected — most recently on March 31, 2025 — so cases are again proceeding in the trial system. Texas's personal injury limitations period is 2 years (Tex. Civ. Prac. & Rem. Code § 16.003), but accrual and discovery-rule questions are fact-specific for cancers diagnosed years after talc use.</p>
</section>

<section id="state-overview">
<h2>What Illinois residents should know</h2>
<p>Illinois residents are not necessarily limited to filing only in Illinois state court. Many mass tort claims may be evaluated by national firms, filed in federal court, coordinated through MDL proceedings, or handled through another legal process.</p>
<p>State law may still matter for deadlines, damages, claim evaluation, and certain procedural issues.</p>
</section>

<section id="state-depo-provera-context">
<h2>Where Illinois Depo-Provera cases are proceeding</h2>
<p>Illinois residents' federal Depo-Provera meningioma cases are transferred into MDL No. 3140 before Judge M. Casey Rodgers in the U.S. District Court for the Northern District of Florida. A small number of Illinois state-court cases (about eight, as of a March 2026 federal status report) are also proceeding, though Illinois has not established a dedicated coordinated state-court docket for Depo-Provera. Because meningioma is often diagnosed years after use, the accrual and product-liability deadline rules summarized in the deadline section on this page can be decisive in an individual case.</p>

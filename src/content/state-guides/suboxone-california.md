<section id="state-overview">
<h2>What California residents should know</h2>
<p>California residents are not necessarily limited to filing only in California state court. Many mass tort claims may be evaluated by national firms, filed in federal court, coordinated through MDL proceedings, or handled through another legal process.</p>
<p>State law may still matter for deadlines, damages, claim evaluation, and certain procedural issues.</p>
</section>

<section id="state-suboxone-context">
<h2>What stands out about Suboxone litigation in California?</h2>
<p>California has been one of the most active state enforcers on Indivior, though not on tooth-decay claims specifically. California&rsquo;s Division of Medi-Cal Fraud and Elder Abuse co-led the multistate negotiation of the April 2021 federal $300 million Indivior Medicaid-fraud settlement (California received approximately $1.64 million from that settlement, in addition to about $2.85 million from the earlier 2019 Reckitt Benckiser $1.4 billion multistate settlement). California also joined the 2023 multistate $102.5 million Suboxone antitrust settlement and served on the executive committee of the July 2024 $86 million settlement in principle.</p>
<p>As of mid-2026, no California state-court verdict on Suboxone tooth-decay claims has been reported. Federal cases filed by California residents are transferred into MDL-3092 in the Northern District of Ohio.</p>
<p>California&rsquo;s personal injury statute of limitations is generally two years (Cal. Civ. Proc. Code &sect; 335.1), with a discovery rule that can apply when an injury and its likely cause are not immediately apparent.</p>

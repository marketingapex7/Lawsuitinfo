<section id="state-suboxone-context">
<h2>What stands out about Suboxone litigation in Ohio?</h2>
<p>Ohio is the host state for the federal Suboxone tooth-decay litigation. MDL-3092 (<em>In re: Suboxone (Buprenorphine/Naloxone) Film Products Liability Litigation</em>) is pending in the Northern District of Ohio (Cleveland) before Judge J. Philip Calabrese, where federal Suboxone tooth-decay cases from across the country are coordinated for pretrial proceedings.</p>
<p>Ohio has also been a lead enforcer against Indivior. Ohio Attorney General Dave Yost was a co-lead negotiator on the 2021 federal $300 million Indivior Medicaid-fraud settlement and announced Ohio&rsquo;s leadership role in the June 2023 multistate $102.5 million Suboxone antitrust settlement, in which Ohio received approximately $5.9 million (the complaint specifically cited Ohio&rsquo;s Valentine Act). Ohio also served on the executive committee for the July 2024 $86 million multistate settlement in principle.</p>
<p>As of mid-2026, no Ohio state-court verdict on the tooth-decay claims has been reported. Ohio&rsquo;s personal injury statute of limitations is generally two years (Ohio Rev. Code &sect; 2305.10(A)), with a discovery rule that can apply to dental injuries that emerge over time.</p>

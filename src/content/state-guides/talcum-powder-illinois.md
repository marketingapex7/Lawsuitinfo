<section id="state-overview">
<h2>What Illinois residents should know</h2>
<p>Illinois residents with talcum powder claims are generally not limited to Illinois state court. Most federal ovarian-cancer cases are coordinated in the multidistrict litigation, <strong>MDL-2738</strong>, before Judge Michael A. Shipp in the U.S. District Court for the District of New Jersey, which held about 68,029 pending actions as of June 1, 2026 — the largest active MDL by pending actions.</p>
<p>Illinois law still matters: the state's filing deadline, discovery rule, damages rules, and procedural law can shape an individual claim even when the case is litigated in the MDL or in state court.</p>
</section>

<section id="state-talcum-powder-context">
<h2>Where Illinois talcum powder cases are handled</h2>
<p>Illinois residents' federal talcum powder claims are coordinated in MDL-2738 before Judge Michael A. Shipp in the U.S. District Court for the District of New Jersey &mdash; the largest active MDL by pending actions (about 68,029 as of June 1, 2026). After Johnson & Johnson's three 'Texas Two-Step' bankruptcy attempts were rejected, most recently on March 31, 2025, tens of thousands of cases returned to active dockets. For Illinois claimants, the practical focus is documentation: the talc product used, years of use, and pathology confirming ovarian cancer or mesothelioma.</p>

<section id="filing-process">
<h2>How Illinois claims proceed</h2>
<p>Most Illinois federal talc cases are filed in or transferred to MDL-2738 in the District of New Jersey for coordinated proceedings, while many ovarian-cancer and mesothelioma cases are also tried in state courts. Johnson & Johnson's three "Texas Two-Step" bankruptcy attempts were all rejected — most recently on March 31, 2025 — so cases are again proceeding in the trial system. Illinois's personal injury limitations period is 2 years (735 ILCS 5/13-202), but accrual and discovery-rule questions are fact-specific for cancers diagnosed years after talc use.</p>

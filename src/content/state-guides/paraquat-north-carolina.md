<h2>What North Carolina residents should know</h2>

<section id="state-paraquat-context">
<h2>What stands out about Paraquat litigation in North Carolina?</h2>
<p>North Carolina has a uniquely direct connection to the defendants. Syngenta Crop Protection LLC's North American headquarters is in Greensboro, North Carolina (Guilford County). Syngenta's principal U.S. place of business is therefore in-state, while paraquat cases filed by NC residents proceed in the federal MDL.</p>
<p>NC residents' federal paraquat cases (filed in the Eastern, Middle, or Western District of North Carolina) are transferred by the JPML into MDL-3004 in the Southern District of Illinois, before Chief Judge Nancy J. Rosenstengel. Paraquat is documented as an approved restricted-use burndown herbicide in NC crop systems per the NC Agricultural Chemicals Manual (NCSU Extension), and NC residents are included in USDA NASS soybean and cotton chemical-use surveys.</p>
<p>As of mid-2026, a review of North Carolina Department of Justice announcements did not surface a state-level paraquat action against Syngenta or Chevron; this is an absence-of-evidence statement rather than a guarantee. North Carolina's product-liability claims are subject to a three-year personal injury period (N.C. Gen. Stat. &sect; 1-52(16)) and a 12-year statute of repose (&sect; 1-46.1).</p>

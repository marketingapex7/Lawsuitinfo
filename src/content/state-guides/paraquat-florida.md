<section id="state-overview">
<h2>What Florida residents should know</h2>
<p>Florida residents are not necessarily limited to filing only in Florida state court. Many mass tort claims may be evaluated by national firms, filed in federal court, coordinated through MDL proceedings, or handled through another legal process.</p>
<p>State law may still matter for deadlines, damages, claim evaluation, and certain procedural issues.</p>
</section>

<section id="state-paraquat-context">
<h2>What stands out about Paraquat litigation in Florida?</h2>
<p>Florida's primary paraquat exposure context is citrus. University of Florida IFAS Extension materials identify paraquat (Gramoxone) alongside glyphosate as a standard non-selective post-emergent contact herbicide in Florida citrus weed management. Paraquat remains a federally restricted-use pesticide that may only be applied by certified applicators.</p>
<p>Federal paraquat cases filed by Florida residents in any of the three Florida federal districts are transferred by the JPML into MDL-3004 (S.D. Illinois, Chief Judge Nancy J. Rosenstengel) for coordinated pretrial proceedings.</p>
<p>As of mid-2026, a review of Florida Department of Agriculture and Consumer Services (FDACS) records and Florida Attorney General press releases did not surface a state-level paraquat action against Syngenta or Chevron; this is an absence-of-evidence statement rather than a guarantee. Florida's product-liability statute of limitations is generally four years (Fla. Stat. &sect; 95.11(3)(d)); accrual, discovery-rule, and repose questions can still change the controlling deadline in an individual case.</p>

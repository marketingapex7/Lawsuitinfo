<section id="state-paraquat-context">
<h2>What stands out about Paraquat litigation in California?</h2>
<p>California is the only state to have enacted a paraquat-specific reevaluation mandate. AB 1963, signed into law on September 27, 2024, requires the California Department of Pesticide Regulation (CDPR) to complete a formal reevaluation of paraquat by January 1, 2029. The 2024 legislative push did not enact an outright ban, but the reevaluation framework is distinct to California.</p>
<p>California also carries a direct defendant connection: Chevron U.S.A. Inc. &mdash; one of the named paraquat defendants &mdash; is headquartered in San Ramon, California. Paraquat is used in California's almond, grape, and other specialty-crop sectors, and remains a federally restricted-use pesticide that may only be applied by certified applicators.</p>
<p>Federal paraquat cases filed by California residents in any of the four California federal districts are transferred by the JPML into MDL-3004 (S.D. Illinois, Chief Judge Nancy J. Rosenstengel) for coordinated pretrial proceedings. As of mid-2026, a review of CDPR enforcement news and California Attorney General press releases did not surface a state-level paraquat action against Syngenta or Chevron; this is an absence-of-evidence statement rather than a guarantee.</p>

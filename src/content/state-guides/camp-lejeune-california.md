<section id="state-overview">
<h2>What California residents should know</h2>
<p>Camp Lejeune claims are federal statutory claims under the Camp Lejeune Justice Act, decided exclusively in the <strong>U.S. District Court for the Eastern District of North Carolina</strong> — regardless of whether the claimant lives in California or anywhere else.</p>
<p><strong>The CLJA filing window closed on August 10, 2024.</strong> This page is for California residents whose claims were filed in time: roughly 407,000 administrative claims and 3,744 lawsuits remain in process, with about $800 million in settlements approved and more than $570 million paid through spring 2026.</p>
</section>

<section id="eligibility">
<h2>Possible eligibility factors</h2>
<ul>
<li>A claim filed with the Navy (or a lawsuit filed in E.D.N.C.) on or before August 10, 2024.</li>
<li>Documented presence at Camp Lejeune for at least 30 cumulative days between August 1, 1953 and December 31, 1987.</li>
<li>A qualifying diagnosis — Elective Option tiers cover kidney, liver, and bladder cancers, leukemia, non-Hodgkin lymphoma, multiple myeloma, Parkinson's disease, kidney disease, and systemic sclerosis.</li>
<li>Updated medical records from California providers, which affect settlement tier placement and offer values.</li>
</ul>
</section>

<section id="filing-process">
<h2>How California claims proceed</h2>
<p>California residents do not file or appear in California courts for these claims. Pending administrative claims are reviewed by the Navy JAG Tort Claims Unit; unresolved claims proceed in the Eastern District of North Carolina, where Track 1 bellwether trials are underway in 2026. Elective Option offers — tiered at roughly $100,000 to $450,000 plus $100,000 for qualifying wrongful-death claims — continue to be extended weekly.</p>
</section>

<section id="state-overview">
<h2>What Ohio residents should know</h2>
<p>Ohio residents with Paragard claims are generally not limited to Ohio state court. Most federal cases are transferred into the multidistrict litigation, <strong>MDL-2974</strong>, before Judge Leigh Martin May in the U.S. District Court for the Northern District of Georgia, which held about 4,071 pending cases as of June 1, 2026.</p>
<p>Ohio law still matters: the state's filing deadline, discovery rule, damages rules, and procedural law can shape an individual claim even when the case is litigated in the MDL.</p>
</section>

<section id="state-paragard-context">
<h2>What Ohio residents should know about the Paragard lawsuits</h2>
<p>Cases filed by Ohio residents in federal court are transferred into <strong>MDL-2974</strong> before Judge Leigh Martin May in the Northern District of Georgia, where about 4,071 cases were pending as of June 1, 2026. The first bellwether trial ended in a defense verdict for Teva in February 2026, and additional bellwether trials are scheduled in 2026; no global settlement exists.</p>
<p>Ohio&rsquo;s personal injury deadline is 2 years (Ohio Rev. Code § 2305.10). Because a broken Paragard is often discovered only when the device fractures during removal, accrual and discovery-rule questions can be important. Ohio's statute includes discovery-based accrual provisions for certain exposure-related claims. Ohio applies a 10-year statute of repose to many product liability claims (§ 2305.10(C)). A lawyer can confirm how the deadline applies to a specific Ohio claim.</p>

<section id="state-overview">
<h2>What Georgia residents should know</h2>
<p>Georgia residents are not necessarily limited to filing only in Georgia state court. Many mass tort claims may be evaluated by national firms, filed in federal court, coordinated through MDL proceedings, or handled through another legal process.</p>
<p>State law may still matter for deadlines, damages, claim evaluation, and certain procedural issues.</p>
</section>

<section id="state-paraquat-context">
<h2>What stands out about Paraquat litigation in Georgia?</h2>
<p>Georgia is one of the country's most agriculturally intensive states for paraquat-relevant crops. Per the USDA NASS 2018 Peanuts Chemical Use Survey, Georgia accounted for about 46.7% of U.S. peanut acreage (about 665,000 of 1.43 million planted acres). Per the USDA NASS 2021 Cotton Chemical Use Survey, Georgia carried about 1.17 million cotton acres (about 10.4% of U.S. cotton acreage). Paraquat is used as a burndown herbicide in both crops where state extension materials approve it.</p>
<p>Federal paraquat cases filed by Georgia residents in the Northern, Middle, or Southern District of Georgia are transferred by the JPML into MDL-3004 (S.D. Illinois, Chief Judge Nancy J. Rosenstengel) for coordinated pretrial proceedings.</p>
<p>Georgia administers the federal restricted-use pesticide program under the Georgia Pesticide Use and Application Act of 1976. As of mid-2026, a review of Georgia Attorney General announcements did not surface a state-level paraquat action against Syngenta or Chevron; this is an absence-of-evidence statement rather than a guarantee. Georgia's personal injury statute of limitations is generally two years (O.C.G.A. &sect; 9-3-33), with a 10-year statute of repose for many product liability claims (&sect; 51-1-11(b)(2)).</p>
